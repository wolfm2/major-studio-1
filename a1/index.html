Notes:
Chosen Graphic: Africa's Great Solar Potential

The graphic contains three core messages:

1) Potential - Africa has above average geographic potential for PV due to the amount 
of solar irradiation it gets relative to other regions.

2) Prosperity & Infrastructure - Regardless of point one, Africa's infrastructural 
build-out is meager compared to prosperous regions with less ultimate potential.  
The implication being that similar African infrastructure would engender similar prosperity. 

3) Programs - Given the enormous untapped potential, the UNDP is engaging in many pilot 
programs to aid in development.


I have chosen to incorporate the above messages into a minimally combinatoric web interface 
aimed at desktops.


Design choices:

The three rhetorical assertions are broken into 3 visually separate panels
to clearly delimit each.

Non-essential maps and irradience ramp are deleted.

The the rhetorical assertions are reordered to build properly on each other.
(I.E.: I switched the first two messages in the graphic)

As arguments go, comparing a continent (Africa) which has suffered under 
colonization to a single prosperous European country (Germany) borders on a 
"hasty generalization" fallacy.   

In attempt to keep the original rhetorical message, the comparisons in the 
first two panels were changed to "Comparing Africa's solar potential in terms 
of core markets in other regions", and "Prosperity creation of solar within 
Africa" respectively to both aid in intelligibility and render them more fair.

The appeal to scientific data-points to engender a sense of authority was 
amplified.

Interactivity was added to allow greater audience to engagement with the data.

The color scheme was kept very similar as it was an effective environmentally
themed palette.

Qualitative data was added to visually typify/clarify UNDP pilot 
programs.
	
Ariel was chosen due to its clean modern look and legibility.
