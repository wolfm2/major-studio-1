What are the risks to women in Nigeria
    when access to clean energy is limited?

Dirty fuel for lighting and cooking is common in Nigeria.[4] As a result the nation's underserved communities have an increased likelihood of many respiratory diseases.


In particular, since cooking is still largely a gendered task, women and their children are subjected to a greater burden of disease when existing infrastructure provides no alternatives.


The UNDP has taken this opprotunity to lead several groundbreaking initiatives[3] that will both support sustainable development goals and, once and for all, address the gender delineated inequities that are a hallmark of dirty fuel.
